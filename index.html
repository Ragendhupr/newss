Today's news headline

Standing as a timeless testament to human ingenuity, the Eiffel Tower is more than just a landmark; it is the very symbol of Paris and France. Completed in just over two years, it was built to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution and to showcase French engineering prowess. Despite its initial temporary status, the tower has endured to become one of the most visited, iconic structures in the world.Designed by engineers Emile Nouguier and Maurice Koechlin, with aesthetic enhancements by Stephen Sauvestre and construction by Gustave Eiffel, the tower utilized groundbreaking wrought-iron construction. At its completion in 1889, it stood at 324 meters (including antennas), surpassing the Washington Monument to become the tallest man-made structure in the world, a title it held for 41 years until 1930. However, it was not initially beloved; many artists and intellectuals criticized the iron structure, calling it an eyesore before it eventually gained widespread admiration.The tower was designed to be dismantled after 20 years, but its survival was secured by its usefulness in scientific experiments and, most importantly, as a telegraph station and radio antenna. It has housed laboratories, a newspaper office, and a theater.
